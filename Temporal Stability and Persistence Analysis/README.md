# Temporal Stability and Persistence Analysis

## Sustained Cortical State Reorganisation

This directory contains figures and processed CSV outputs examining the temporal persistence of cortical state reorganisation during the modulation condition.

To determine whether the changes identified in the coherence and entropy analyses were transient or sustained, temporal stability metrics were calculated from sliding-window entropy time series across cortical regions and frequency bands. Two complementary measures were evaluated: the autocorrelation decay constant (τ₁/e), which quantifies the persistence of regional activity patterns, and the mean dwell time above baseline, which measures how long a region remained in an elevated dynamical state.

The analyses demonstrated selective increases in temporal persistence rather than uniform stabilisation across the cortex. Several regional-frequency combinations exhibited substantially longer autocorrelation decay constants and extended dwell times, indicating that the modulation preferentially stabilised specific functional networks while preserving flexibility in others.

These findings extend the coherence and entropy analyses by demonstrating that the observed cortical reorganisation was maintained over meaningful periods rather than occurring as isolated or transient events. The combined evidence suggests that neuromodulation influences not only network coordination and information dynamics, but also the temporal stability of the resulting cortical states.

Together, the coherence, entropy, complexity, and temporal persistence analyses support the interpretation that the modulation induces a coordinated systems-level reorganisation of cortical dynamics characterised by adaptive network coordination, frequency-specific information processing, and sustained neural state stability.
