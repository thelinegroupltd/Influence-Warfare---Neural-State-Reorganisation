# Entropy and Complexity Metrics

## Information Dynamics and Cortical Organisation

This directory contains figures examining changes in cortical information dynamics through Shannon entropy and Lempel–Ziv (LZ76) complexity analyses.

Shannon entropy quantifies the variability of spectral signal distributions, while Lempel–Ziv complexity estimates the temporal diversity and compressibility of neural activity. Together, these complementary measures provide insight into how neural dynamics are reorganised during modulation.

Relative to the baseline resting-state condition, the modulation session exhibited higher Shannon entropy across all analysed frequency bands, indicating changes in the distribution of spectral information. In contrast, Lempel–Ziv complexity showed frequency-dependent behaviour, with reductions observed in several bands, little change in others, and a modest increase within the alpha band. This dissociation suggests that greater spectral variability did not necessarily correspond to greater temporal complexity.

Importantly, these findings should not be interpreted as the brain becoming simply "more random" or "more active." Instead, they are consistent with a reorganisation of cortical dynamics in which informational content and temporal structure become selectively reconfigured across frequency bands.

Viewed alongside the coherence analyses, the entropy and complexity results support the interpretation that neuromodulation alters the organisation of cortical activity rather than merely changing oscillatory power. The combined evidence is consistent with an adaptive shift toward a different dynamical operating regime characterised by coordinated large-scale interactions and frequency-specific reorganisation of neural information processing.
